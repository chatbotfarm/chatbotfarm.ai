Common Booking Categories

The Digital Office starts by clarifying the booking type and collecting the essentials.

  • Private events (birthdays, celebrations)
  • Weddings and receptions
  • Corporate events
  • Clubs / venues / recurring nights
  • Festivals and ticketed events
  • Press / collaborations / sponsorship inquiries

Structured Intake Fields

Instead of back-and-forth messaging, the system captures consistent details so you can quote quickly and accurately.

  • Event date and time window
  • Venue name + address (or city)
  • Event type
  • Estimated attendance
  • Budget range (optional, based on your preference)
  • Contact name + phone/email
  • Special notes (set length, equipment, travel, genre preferences)

Workflows That Close More Bookings

Once the intake is captured, automation handles the repetitive steps that typically get missed.

  • Send a confirmation message acknowledging the request
  • Route the inquiry to the correct pipeline lane (wedding, corporate, club, etc.)
  • Send a link for scheduling a call (if needed)
  • Trigger follow-up reminders if the lead goes quiet
  • Log all conversation history in one place

The goal is simple: keep every lead moving forward until it’s booked or closed out.

When Human Judgment Is Needed

The goal is not to replace your team—it’s to replace repetitive tasks with workflows so you can focus on performance and revenue.

When the AI is unsure, the system escalates cleanly:

  • A memo is sent to the assigned team member
  • The full conversation context is included
  • A clear question explains what decision is needed (pricing, availability, contract terms)
